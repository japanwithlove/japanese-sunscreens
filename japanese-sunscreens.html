How to Choose Japanese Sunscreens For Your Skin Type

It's possible to feel intimidated by the packaging in Japanese. In this article, I'll break it down for you.

It's only a handful of things that you should know and it's quite simple.

These are the 3 things you should be aware of Japanese Sunscreens:

1. SPF / PF

Select the best SPF and PA.

These 2 metrics will be on Japanese sunscreen labels.

  • SPF (10-50)
  • PA (+ - ++++)

These metrics show how strong the sun protection that you can get from sunscreen.

It is important to be able to interpret these two metrics in order to find the best one for you.

SPF (Sun Protection Factor - Protect against sunburn

  • This number represents the amount of protection you receive from UVB rays. The scale ranges from 10 to 50+.
  • It simply indicates how long it takes for your skin to turn red from UVB. It's likely that your skin will turn red if it is less than 10 minutes.
  • The higher the number, the more you can protect your skin against sunburn.

PA (Protection grade of UVA) protects the skin from premature ageing.

  • The number of + signs in your name indicates how protected you are from UVA rays. It is a scale from + - ++++.
  • It simply indicates how long it takes for your skin to turn dark from UVA. If the + is displayed, your skin will turn dark quickly.
  • Your skin will be less likely to turn dark and prematurely if it has more +.

Notice: UVB (or UVA) are the two types UV rays that reach the earth's surface.

  • UVA - penetrates deeper into the skin, and plays a larger role in premature skin aging including wrinkles (photoaging).
  • UVB - Responsible for sunburns and the most important role in cancer.

The UVA rays are 500 times stronger than the UVB rays.

It is important to select a sunscreen that protects against both UVB (check SPF), and UVA (check +).

Here are some guidelines to help you choose the right strength.

You can go out for around an hour, whether you are walking, shopping, or commuting.

SPF 10-30 / PA++ - +++

Relaxing on the beach, in the sun (between 10 and 5 in summertime).

- SPF 30-50 / PA ++++

This is also a good reminder:

  • The greater the number, the better, but it will also mean that the skin burden will increase.
  • Both types are recommended.
  • One for daily use, one for summertime/recreational purposes.

2. Texture

You can choose the texture based on the occasion and skin type.

There are five main types of texture found in Japanese Sunscreens. You can choose the type that suits your needs.

Milk

  • It is easy to spread, smooth on the skin and non-sticky
  • Many are gentle and non-chemical for the skin.

Suitable for sensitive skin and face

Cream

  • Moisturising
  • Waterproof (resistant to water)

Ideal for outdoor swimming and other leisure activities in the sun

Gel / Essence

  • It is easy to feel light and comfortable.
  • It is less likely that you will get white cast

- Ideal if you are primarily looking for lightness and ease of use

Mist spray

  • It is very light and easy to use.
  • It can be used in areas that are hard to reach (hair, scalp, etc.).

This product is great for hair and scalp touch-ups.

Powder

  • It is very light and easy to use.
  • Can be used to build a foundation

This is a great tool for quick touch-ups, shopping and walking.

3. UV Reflecting or absorbing

Is the sun reflecting (physical sunscreen) or absorption (chemical sunscreen) of ingredients?

There are two types of sunscreen: chemical or physical.

There are two ways to block sunlight.

Each has its own benefits and unique properties in protecting the skin against UV rays.

Physical sunscreen

  • Minerals that are reflected off the sun and sit on top of skin.
  • Moisturising sunscreens are more effective than those with chemical sunscreens
  • It can make the skin look a bit oily.

It doesn't absorb directly into skin, making it a better choice for sensitive skin.

Chemical sunscreen

  • Ingredients that absorb and convert UV rays into heat
  • It is usually lightweight and does not leave a white cast.
  • More water/sweat-resistant

Ideal for outdoor activities, and suitable for all skin types

Keep in mind that most sunscreens found in your local drugstore or pharmacy are chemical sunscreens.If you have sensitive skin, however, it is worth looking for a physical sunscreen.
